For IT companies, a workspace is much more than just desks and chairs. It requires robust server infrastructure, enterprise-grade internet redundancy, biometric security, and dedicated meeting pods for daily scrums. 

Historically, securing this level of infrastructure meant signing a 5-to-9-year traditional commercial lease and sinking massive capital expenditure (CAPEX) into fit-outs and IT deployment. Today, fast-growing tech companies are switching to the **[Managed Office](/managed-office)** model.

Here is why a Managed Office is the perfect operational model for IT companies.

---

## 1. Zero CAPEX on Fit-Outs and IT Infrastructure

When you lease a bare-shell office, you are responsible for everything: HVAC installation, false ceilings, raised flooring for server rooms, structured cabling, and enterprise networking gear. For an office seating 50-100 employees, this can easily cost tens of lakhs in upfront capital.

A Managed Office converts this massive CAPEX into a predictable monthly OPEX (Operational Expenditure). The workspace provider handles the entire build-out to your exact specifications. You simply walk in on day one, plug in your laptops, and start coding.

## 2. Enterprise-Grade Security and Compliance

IT companies, especially those dealing in fintech, healthcare (HIPAA compliance), or enterprise SaaS, have strict data security and physical access requirements. 

A standard coworking space rarely meets these requirements because the network is shared. A Managed Office, however, provides:
* **Dedicated Server Rooms:** Secure, climate-controlled environments for your on-premise hardware.
* **Isolated Networks:** Dedicated VLANs, static IPs, and firewall configurations customized to your IT policy.
* **Physical Access Control:** Biometric or RFID access restricted exclusively to your employees.

## 3. Unmatched Scalability for Hyper-Growth

IT companies experience rapid scaling phases. If you sign a traditional 5-year lease for a 5,000 sq ft office, you are paying for empty space while you grow into it. Worse, if you outgrow it in two years, you are trapped in the lease.

Managed Offices offer unparalleled flexibility. Providers like WeeSpaces allow you to start with a customized floor plan for 50 employees and scale up to [office space for 100 employees](/blog/office-space-for-100-employees) within the same building as your headcount expands. 

## 4. Total Focus on Core Operations

Running a traditional office requires an entire facility management team. Someone has to deal with the internet service provider when the connection drops, handle housekeeping, manage the cafeteria, and fix the air conditioning. 

In a Managed Office, facility management is entirely outsourced. Your HR and Operations teams are free to focus on recruiting top talent and driving company culture, while the workspace operator handles the day-to-day logistics.
