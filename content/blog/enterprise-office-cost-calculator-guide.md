When a CFO or Operations Director begins planning a new corporate headquarters or regional hub, the first question is always: *"How much is this going to cost?"*

If you only look at the base commercial rent (the per-square-foot cost quoted by the landlord), you are missing up to 60% of the true financial picture. Budgeting for an enterprise office requires factoring in capital expenditures (CAPEX), operational expenditures (OPEX), hidden compliance costs, and the cost of inflexibility.

This guide will help you understand the true metrics behind enterprise office costs.

---

## 1. The CAPEX Reality: The Fit-Out

When leasing a traditional bare-shell commercial property, the landlord provides a concrete floor and a ceiling. You have to build the rest.

A standard enterprise fit-out includes:
* HVAC (Air Conditioning) installation
* Electrical wiring, power backup (UPS/DG sets), and structured networking
* Raised flooring for server rooms
* Glass partitions, acoustic paneling, and doors
* Workstations, ergonomic chairs, and pantry appliances

**The Cost:** Depending on the finish (standard vs. premium), a custom fit-out in cities like Kochi or Coimbatore will cost between **₹1,500 to ₹3,000 per square foot**. For a 10,000 sq ft [office space for 100 employees](/blog/office-space-for-100-employees), that is an upfront capital sink of ₹1.5 Crore to ₹3 Crore before a single employee walks through the door.

*Note: In a [Build-to-Suit Managed Office](/blog/build-to-suit-office-coimbatore), this CAPEX is reduced to zero, as the provider absorbs the fit-out cost and amortizes it into your monthly rent.*

## 2. The Hidden OPEX: Facility Management

Base rent is just the beginning. To calculate the true monthly cost of running an office, you must add:

* **CAM (Common Area Maintenance):** Often an additional ₹10 to ₹15 per sq ft charged by the building association.
* **Utilities:** Electricity for a 100-person office running heavy IT infrastructure and HVAC can be enormous.
* **Internet:** Dedicated enterprise lease lines with redundant backups.
* **Staffing:** Salaries for security guards, housekeeping staff, a facility manager, and a receptionist.
* **Consumables:** Coffee, tea, printer ink, and cleaning supplies.

**The Cost:** Running an office independently usually adds an additional **30% to 40%** on top of your base rent in hidden OPEX.

## 3. The Density Metric: Cost Per Seat vs Cost Per Sq Ft

Commercial real estate is leased by the square foot, but businesses operate by the headcount. 

To accurately compare the cost of a traditional lease versus a [Managed Office](/managed-office), you must calculate the **Cost Per Seat**.

**How to calculate it:**
`(Monthly Rent + Monthly OPEX + Amortized CAPEX) ÷ Number of Usable Desks = True Cost Per Seat`

A traditional lease might look cheaper on a per-square-foot basis, but because you have to dedicate 20% of the floor plan to corridors, reception areas, and electrical rooms, your *usable* desk density is lower, driving the Cost Per Seat up significantly.

## 4. The Cost of Inflexibility (The "Ghost Space" Tax)

This is the hardest cost to put on a spreadsheet, but it is often the most expensive. 

When you sign a 5-year traditional lease, you must lease enough space for your *projected* headcount in Year 3. This means in Year 1 and Year 2, you are paying rent, electricity, and maintenance on empty desks. This is the "Ghost Space" tax.

Conversely, if you outgrow the space in Year 3, breaking the lease agreement incurs massive legal and financial penalties. (Read our [Managed vs Traditional Lease comparison](/blog/managed-office-vs-traditional-lease) for a deeper dive into lock-ins).
