## 1. How Much Space Do You Actually Need?

The traditional real estate rule of thumb is 100 square feet per employee. For a 100-person team, that translates to a **10,000 sq ft floor plate**. 

However, modern workspace design has changed this calculus:
* **The Hybrid Factor:** If your company operates on a hybrid model (e.g., 3 days in the office), you might only need 60-70 physical desks for 100 employees, utilizing a hot-desking or hoteling strategy.
* **Density Requirements:** A call center or BPO can operate efficiently at 50-70 sq ft per employee. A high-end software development firm or design agency might require 120-150 sq ft to accommodate large workstations, breakout zones, and multiple meeting rooms.

## 2. Location Strategy in Coimbatore

Where you locate your 100-person office in Coimbatore will dramatically impact your talent acquisition, brand perception, and operational costs.

* **Avinashi Road & RS Puram:** The premium commercial heart of the city. Ideal for corporate headquarters, client-facing operations, and financial services.
* **Saravanampatti & Tidel Park Area:** The undisputed IT corridor of Coimbatore. If you are a tech company competing for top engineering talent, this is where you need to be.
* **Peelamedu:** A great middle-ground offering excellent connectivity to the airport and major educational institutions (perfect for campus recruiting).

## 3. The Problem with Traditional Leases

Leasing a 10,000 sq ft bare-shell property in Coimbatore comes with severe hidden costs:
1. **The Build-Out:** Designing, partitioning, wiring, and furnishing a space of this size can easily cost ₹15,000 to ₹25,000 per sq ft. That is a massive upfront capital expenditure (CAPEX).
2. **Time to Market:** A custom fit-out for 100 employees will take 3 to 6 months of dealing with contractors, architects, and municipal approvals before you can move in.
3. **The 9-Year Trap:** Commercial landlords typically demand long-term lock-ins (3 to 5 years minimum). If your team grows to 200 in two years, you are trapped in a space that is too small.
