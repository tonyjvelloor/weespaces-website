When transitioning your team out of a home office or moving to a new city, flexible workspaces are the obvious choice. But once you walk into a premium workspace provider like WeeSpaces, you face a critical decision: Do you rent [Dedicated Desks](/coworking-space) on the open coworking floor, or do you lease a lockable [Private Office](/private-office)?

Both options offer immense flexibility compared to traditional leases, but they serve very different operational needs. This guide breaks down the pros, cons, and hidden factors of each.

---

## The Open-Plan Coworking Floor

Coworking involves renting desks (either "Hot Desks" or "Dedicated Desks") in a large, shared, open-plan environment alongside other companies, freelancers, and remote workers.

### The Pros:
* **Cost Efficiency:** This is the most affordable way to get your team into a premium commercial building. You pay purely for the desks you occupy.
* **Networking & Serendipity:** Your lead developer might be sitting next to a cloud architect from another startup. The networking opportunities and cross-pollination of ideas in open-plan spaces are unmatched.
* **Vibrant Energy:** Open floors are bustling with activity. For small teams of 2 to 4 people, this energy can cure the isolation of working from home.

### The Cons:
* **Noise and Distractions:** While premium spaces have acoustic treatments, you cannot control the volume of the sales team sitting three desks away.
* **Privacy:** If you are discussing sensitive financials, HR issues, or proprietary code, an open floor is not secure. You will need to constantly book [Meeting Rooms](/meeting-room) for private conversations.

## The Lockable Private Office

A [Private Office](/private-office) is exactly what it sounds like: a fully enclosed, lockable room dedicated exclusively to your team, located *within* the larger flexible workspace facility.

### The Pros:
* **Total Privacy & Security:** You control who enters. You can leave sensitive documents on the desk, install your own local servers, and have confidential conversations without booking a meeting room.
* **Customization and Culture:** A private office allows you to build your own micro-culture. You can display your branding, set your own operational rules, and foster deep team focus.
* **Access to Shared Amenities:** You get the privacy of a traditional office, but you still have free access to the shared community lounges, cafeterias, and networking events right outside your door.

### The Cons:
* **Higher Cost:** Because you are paying for the walls, the door, and the dedicated square footage, a private office has a higher Cost Per Seat than a dedicated desk on the open floor.
* **Space Constraints:** If you rent a 10-person private office and hire an 11th person, you have to move to a larger office (though providers like WeeSpaces make this transition seamless).

## How to Make the Decision

**Choose Open-Plan Coworking If:**
1. You are a solo founder, a freelancer, or a very small team (1-4 people).
2. Your work does not involve handling highly sensitive client data (e.g., healthcare or fintech).
3. Capital conservation is your absolute highest priority.
4. You actively want to network with other startups to find co-founders, clients, or inspiration.

**Choose a Private Office If:**
1. Your team is larger than 5 people.
2. You frequently handle confidential information, conduct interviews, or have strict client data-compliance rules.
3. Your team requires deep, uninterrupted focus (e.g., intensive software development or financial analysis).
4. You want to establish a distinct company culture while avoiding the CAPEX of a [Managed Office](/managed-office).
