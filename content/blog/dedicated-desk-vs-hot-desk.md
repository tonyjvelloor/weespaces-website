You've decided to move out of the home office and into a modern [Coworking Space](/coworking-space). You look at the pricing page of a premium provider like WeeSpaces, and you are immediately faced with a choice: **Hot Desk** or **Dedicated Desk**.

Both options give you access to high-speed internet, endless coffee, and a vibrant community of professionals. So what exactly is the difference, and which one should you choose?

This quick guide breaks down the mechanics, pros, and cons of both coworking models.

---

## What is a Hot Desk?

A Hot Desk (sometimes called a Flexi-Desk) is the ultimate flexible workspace solution. 

With this membership, you do not own a specific desk. When you arrive at the workspace, you choose any available desk in the designated "Hot Desking" zone. You plug in your laptop, work for the day, and when you leave, you take all your belongings with you. The desk is cleared for someone else to use tomorrow.

### The Pros:
* **Maximum Affordability:** Hot desking is always the most budget-friendly membership option.
* **Dynamic Environment:** Because you sit in a different spot every day, you constantly meet new people and experience different views of the workspace.
* **Ultimate Flexibility:** Perfect for digital nomads or remote workers who only need to come into the office 2 or 3 days a week.

### The Cons:
* **No Storage:** You cannot leave your dual monitors, ergonomic keyboard, or files at the office overnight. You must carry your setup in your backpack every day.
* **Unpredictability:** While premium spaces manage their capacity carefully, arriving late on a busy Tuesday might mean you don't get your "favorite" spot by the window.

## What is a Dedicated Desk?

A Dedicated Desk is exactly what it sounds like: a specific desk on the open coworking floor that is assigned exclusively to you. No one else can sit there, even when you are not in the office.

### The Pros:
* **Your Own Setup:** Because the desk is yours 24/7, you can leave your expensive monitors, mechanical keyboards, plants, and family photos on the desk. 
* **Storage Provided:** Dedicated desks almost always come with a lockable filing cabinet or pedestal to securely store sensitive documents and equipment overnight.
* **Routine and Focus:** For people who thrive on routine, going to the exact same workstation every day reduces friction and helps trigger "deep work" mode.
* **Compliance Ready:** If you need to register for GST or MCA compliance, you generally need at least a Dedicated Desk (or a [Virtual Office](/virtual-office) package) to receive the necessary legal documentation (NOC and Rental Agreement).

### The Cons:
* **Higher Cost:** Because the workspace operator cannot sell that desk to anyone else, a Dedicated Desk costs more than a Hot Desk.
* **Static Environment:** You sit next to the same people every day. If you prefer constant environmental shifts, a Dedicated Desk might feel too stationary.

---

## Which One Should You Choose?

**Choose a Hot Desk If:**
* You are a freelancer or solo consultant who travels frequently.
* You work primarily from a laptop and don't need external monitors or heavy equipment.
* You only plan to visit the office a few days a week.
* Cost is your primary determining factor.

**Choose a Dedicated Desk If:**
* You are a small team (2 to 4 people) who needs to sit together and collaborate daily.
* You require a dual-monitor setup or work with heavy hardware.
* You need to leave physical documents or prototypes at the office securely.
* You need to register your company at the address for legal or GST compliance.

### Upgrade Whenever You're Ready

The defining feature of a great coworking space is agility. Start with a Hot Desk to test out the community. When you realize you want to leave your monitor overnight, you can instantly upgrade to a Dedicated Desk. When you hire three more people, you can move your team into a lockable [Private Office](/private-office).
